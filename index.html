While plants are excellent for aesthetics and psychological well-being, they are not an effective solution for removing the most harmful airborne pollutants in a typical office or school. The difference between biology and engineered technology is significant:

  • Ineffective Scale for Particle Removal: To match the air cleaning rate of a standard building's ventilation system, you would need an impractical 100 to 1,000 plants per square meter. This doesn't even approach the efficacy of a high-efficiency HEPA filter. [7]
  • Risk of Contamination: Poorly maintained plant soil can become a breeding ground for mold and bacteria, inadvertently introducing new biological contaminants into your environment.
  • Proven HEPA Efficacy: In contrast, the True HEPA filters in our service are certified to capture over 99.97% of harmful airborne particles, including dust, germs, and viruses on droplets, in a single pass.

The Bottom Line: For a verifiably healthy indoor environment, engineered HEPA filtration is the only solution that provides the necessary efficacy, reliability, and measurable performance.

Our service uses purifiers with True HEPA filters, which are certified to capture 99.97% of particles at 0.3 microns (¬µm). Due to the physics of filtration (interception, impaction, and diffusion), they are highly effective across a broad spectrum of particle sizes, including microbiologicals. [4]

Bacteria Filtration

Most airborne bacteria (typically 0.5-5.0 ¬µm) are well within the capture range, including pathogens like Staphylococcus, Streptococcus, and the droplet nuclei that carry Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Virus Filtration

While individual viruses are tiny (~0.1 ¬µm), they travel on much larger respiratory droplets and aerosols that are easily captured. For the smallest free-floating particles, a mechanism called diffusion ensures they collide with and are captured by the filter fibers. This makes our system effective against Influenza, Coronaviruses (like SARS-CoV-2), and Rhinoviruses.
